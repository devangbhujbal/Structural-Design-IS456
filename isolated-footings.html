1. What is an Isolated Footing?

Definition
An isolated footing (also called a spread footing or pad footing) is an individual foundation provided beneath a single column. It spreads the column load over a larger area of soil so that the soil pressure does not exceed the safe bearing capacity of the ground.

Isolated footings are the most common foundation type in buildings where the soil has adequate bearing capacity and columns are not too closely spaced. They are governed by IS 456 : 2000, Clause 34 and soil design by IS 1904.

The footing acts as an inverted beam or slab — the upward soil pressure causes bending at the face of the column, and the footing must be designed for flexure, one-way shear, and two-way (punching) shear.

4. Safe Bearing Capacity and Net Soil Pressure

The safe bearing capacity (SBC) $q_{sbc}$ is the maximum pressure the soil can safely carry without shear failure or excessive settlement. It is determined from soil investigation (IS 1904) and is an unfactored (service) value.

Key Point — SBC is a Service Load Concept
The footing area is sized using service (unfactored) loads against SBC, because SBC already incorporates a factor of safety. However, once the footing size is fixed, the structural design (depth, steel) uses factored loads and factored soil pressure.
$$q_{net} = q_{sbc} - \gamma_s \cdot D_f$$
Net SBC after deducting overburden. $\gamma_s$ = unit weight of soil ≈ 18 kN/m³; $D_f$ = depth of founding level. Often $q_{net} \approx q_{sbc}$ for simplicity in preliminary design.

5. Footing Plan Area

The plan area is sized so that the soil pressure under service loads does not exceed the net SBC:

$$A_f = \frac{P + W_f}{q_{net}}$$
$P$ = service column load; $W_f$ = self-weight of footing (approx. 10% of $P$ as first estimate); $q_{net}$ = net SBC

For a square footing: $L = B = \sqrt{A_f}$. Round up to the nearest 50 mm or 100 mm.

For a rectangular footing: Choose $L/B$ ratio to suit column dimensions or site constraints.

Practical Approach
For simplicity, take $A_f = P / q_{net}$ (ignoring footing self-weight), then increase the dimensions slightly (round up). The footing self-weight is modest compared to the column load for most practical cases.

6. Net Factored Upward Pressure for Structural Design

Once the footing dimensions are fixed, the net factored upward pressure (used for structural design) is:

$$q_u = \frac{P_u}{L \times B}$$
$P_u = 1.5P$ = factored column load. $L \times B$ = actual footing area provided. Units: kN/m²
Two Different Pressures — Don't Confuse Them
  • For sizing the footing: use service load $P$ and compare with $q_{net}$ (SBC)
  • For structural design (depth, steel): use factored load $P_u = 1.5P$ and compute $q_u = P_u/(L \times B)$

7. Depth of Footing — Two-Way Shear (Punching)

The depth of the footing is primarily governed by two-way (punching) shear. Punching shear occurs along a perimeter at $d/2$ from the column face. The footing must be thick enough so that concrete alone resists this shear without any stirrups.

Fig. 3 — Punching shear perimeter at $d/2$ from column face; one-way shear section at $d$ from column face
IS 456 : 2000 — Clause 31.6
The nominal shear stress on the punching perimeter shall not exceed $k_s \cdot \tau_c$ where $k_s = (0.5 + \beta_c) \leq 1.0$ and $\tau_c = 0.25\sqrt{f_{ck}}$.
$$\tau_c = 0.25 \sqrt{f_{ck}} \quad \text{(MPa)}$$ $$k_s = 0.5 + \beta_c \leq 1.0 \quad \text{where } \beta_c = \frac{\text{short side of column}}{\text{long side of column}}$$
For square columns: $\beta_c = 1$, so $k_s = 0.5 + 1.0 = 1.5$, but capped at 1.0. Hence $\tau_{c,\text{punch}} = 0.25\sqrt{f_{ck}}$.

Punching Shear Force and Check:

$$V_{punch} = q_u \left[L \cdot B - (a + d)(b + d)\right]$$ $$\tau_v = \frac{V_{punch}}{b_o \cdot d} \leq k_s \cdot \tau_c$$
$b_o = 2(a + b + 2d)$ = perimeter of critical section; $a, b$ = column dimensions. IS 456 Cl. 31.6.1

Design Approach — Find $d$ from Punching Shear:

Rearrange the punching shear inequality to solve for the minimum required $d$. This typically requires an iterative or trial-and-error approach since $d$ appears on both sides of the equation.

Trial-and-Error for Depth
Assume $d$ (e.g., $d = (L-a)/4$ as a first trial), compute both $V_{punch}$ and $\tau_v$, and check against $\tau_c$. Increase $d$ if $\tau_v > k_s \tau_c$.

8. One-Way Shear Check

One-way (beam) shear is checked on a section at a distance $d$ from the face of the column. The shear force on this section per unit width is:

$$V_u = q_u \times B \times \left(\frac{L - a}{2} - d\right)$$
One-way shear force at section $d$ from column face (for bending in the long direction). For square footings: $L = B$.
$$\tau_v = \frac{V_u}{B \cdot d} \leq \tau_c$$
Nominal shear stress — $\tau_c$ from IS 456 Table 19 based on $p_t$ and $f_{ck}$. No shear reinforcement in footings — increase $d$ if check fails.

9. Bending Moment and Steel Design

The critical section for bending moment is at the face of the column. The cantilever projection beyond the column face acts as an inverted cantilever loaded by upward soil pressure.

$$M_u = q_u \cdot B \cdot \frac{\left(\dfrac{L-a}{2}\right)^2}{2}$$
Bending moment at the face of the column — IS 456:2000, Cl. 34.2.3.2. For a square footing: same in both directions if $L = B$ and $a = b$.

The steel is then designed as a 1 m wide strip (or for the full width) using the rectangular section formula:

$$A_{st} = \frac{0.5 f_{ck}}{f_y}\left[1 - \sqrt{1 - \frac{4.6 M_u}{f_{ck} \cdot B \cdot d^2}}\right] B \cdot d$$
Total steel area in the full footing width $B$. Minimum steel = 0.12% of $B \times D$ (for HYSD bars).
IS 456 : 2000 — Clause 34.3
For square footings, the reinforcement extending in each direction shall be distributed uniformly across the full width. For rectangular footings, a central band of width equal to the short footing dimension shall contain a higher proportion of the long-direction steel (IS 456 Cl. 34.3.1(c)).

10. Development Length Check

The tension bars in the footing must have sufficient length beyond the critical section (column face) to develop their full design stress. The available development length is:

$$L_{available} = \frac{L - a}{2} - \text{end cover}$$
Available length from column face to end of footing bar, minus end cover (typically 50 mm for footings).
$$L_d = \frac{\phi \cdot \sigma_s}{4 \tau_{bd}} = \frac{0.87 f_y \cdot \phi}{4 \tau_{bd}}$$
Required development length — IS 456:2000, Cl. 26.2.1. $\tau_{bd}$ = design bond stress from IS 456 Table 6.
Concrete Grade$\tau_{bd}$ for plain bars (MPa)$\tau_{bd}$ for deformed bars (MPa)
M201.21.2 × 1.6 = 1.92
M251.41.4 × 1.6 = 2.24
M301.51.5 × 1.6 = 2.40
Development Length for Common Bar Sizes (Fe 415, M20)
$L_d = \dfrac{0.87 \times 415 \times \phi}{4 \times 1.92} = \dfrac{361.05\phi}{7.68} = 47\phi$
  • 12 mm bar: $L_d = 47 \times 12 = 564$ mm ≈ 565 mm
  • 16 mm bar: $L_d = 47 \times 16 = 752$ mm
  • 20 mm bar: $L_d = 47 \times 20 = 940$ mm

12. Step-by-Step Design Procedure

  • Given Data Service load $P$, column size $a \times b$, SBC $q_{sbc}$, $f_{ck}$, $f_y$.
  • Find Net SBC and Footing Area $q_{net} \approx q_{sbc} - \gamma_s D_f$. $A_f = P/q_{net}$. Find $L$ and $B$. Round up.
  • Compute Factored Load and Net Upward Pressure $P_u = 1.5P$. $q_u = P_u/(L \times B)$.
  • Assume Trial Depth $d$; Check Two-Way (Punching) Shear Trial: $d \approx (L-a)/4$. Compute $V_{punch}$ and $\tau_v = V_{punch}/(b_o \cdot d)$. Check $\tau_v \leq 0.25\sqrt{f_{ck}}$. Revise $d$ if needed.
  • Check One-Way Shear $V_u = q_u \cdot B \cdot [(L-a)/2 - d]$. $\tau_v = V_u/(B \cdot d)$. Check against $\tau_c$ from IS 456 Table 19.
  • Calculate Bending Moment and Design Steel $M_u = q_u \cdot B \cdot [(L-a)/2]^2 / 2$. Find $A_{st}$ using rectangular formula. Check minimum steel.
  • Select Bars and Check Spacing Choose dia and number of bars. Spacing $\leq \min(3d, 300)$ mm.
  • Check Development Length $L_{available} = (L-a)/2 - 50 \geq L_d$. If not, reduce bar diameter.
  • Overall Depth $D$ and Final Check $D = d + 50 + \phi/2$ (cover = 50 mm for footings). Check $D \geq 150$ mm at edges.
  • Draw Detailing Sketch Plan showing bar arrangement; section showing $D$, $d$, cover, PCC layer, column bars into footing.

13. Worked Example

Problem Statement

Design a square isolated footing for the following data:

  • Service load from column $P$ = 800 kN
  • Column size = 400 mm × 400 mm
  • Safe bearing capacity of soil $q_{sbc}$ = 150 kN/m²
  • Concrete: M20 ($f_{ck}$ = 20 MPa)
  • Steel: Fe 415 ($f_y$ = 415 MPa)
  • Depth of founding level: 1.5 m below GL (unit weight of soil = 18 kN/m³)
Step 1 — Net SBC and Footing Area

Net SBC: $q_{net} = 150 - 18 \times 1.5 = 150 - 27 = 123$ kN/m²

Including self-weight of footing (≈10% of $P$): $A_f = \dfrac{P \times 1.1}{q_{net}} = \dfrac{800 \times 1.1}{123} = \dfrac{880}{123}$

$A_f = 7.15$ m². Side $= \sqrt{7.15} = 2.675$ m → round up to $L = B = 2.8$ m

Footing size: 2.8 m × 2.8 m (= 2800 mm × 2800 mm)
Step 2 — Factored Load and Net Upward Pressure

$P_u = 1.5 \times 800 = 1200$ kN

$q_u = \dfrac{1200}{2.8 \times 2.8} = \dfrac{1200}{7.84}$

$q_u$ = 153.06 kN/m²
Step 3 — Trial Depth from Punching Shear

Permissible punching shear stress: $\tau_c = 0.25\sqrt{20} = 0.25 \times 4.472 = 1.118$ MPa

$k_s = 0.5 + \beta_c = 0.5 + 1.0 = 1.5 \leq 1.0$ → $k_s = 1.0$; $k_s\tau_c = 1.118$ MPa

Trial $d = 400$ mm. Punching perimeter side = $400 + 400 = 800$ mm; $b_o = 4 \times 800 = 3200$ mm

Punching area avoided = $(a+d)^2 = 800^2 = 640000$ mm²

$V_{punch} = q_u [L \cdot B - (a+d)^2] = 153.06 \times [2.8^2 - 0.8^2] = 153.06 \times (7.84 - 0.64) = 153.06 \times 7.20$

$= 1102.0$ kN

$\tau_v = \dfrac{1102.0 \times 10^3}{3200 \times 400} = \dfrac{1102000}{1280000}$

$\tau_v$ = 0.861 MPa < $k_s\tau_c$ (1.118 MPa) ✓ — $d$ = 400 mm is adequate for punching shear
Step 4 — One-Way Shear Check

Projection from column face = $(2800 - 400)/2 = 1200$ mm

Section at $d$ = 400 mm from column face; remaining projection = $1200 - 400 = 800$ mm

$V_u = q_u \times B \times (1200 - 400)/1000 = 153.06 \times 2.8 \times 0.8 = 342.85$ kN

$\tau_v = \dfrac{342850}{2800 \times 400} = \dfrac{342850}{1120000}$

$\tau_v$ = 0.306 MPa

Assume $p_t \approx 0.3\%$ (to be verified). From IS 456 Table 19, M20, $p_t = 0.3\%$: $\tau_c \approx 0.38$ MPa

$\tau_v$ (0.306) < $\tau_c$ (0.38) ✓ — One-way shear OK

Step 5 — Bending Moment

Critical section at face of column; cantilever projection = $(L-a)/2 = (2800-400)/2 = 1200$ mm = 1.2 m

$M_u = q_u \times B \times \dfrac{(projection)^2}{2} = 153.06 \times 2.8 \times \dfrac{1.2^2}{2}$

$= 153.06 \times 2.8 \times 0.72 = 308.6$ kN·m

$M_u$ = 308.6 kN·m (for full footing width of 2800 mm)
Step 6 — Steel Design

Using $b = B = 2800$ mm, $d = 400$ mm, $M_u = 308.6 \times 10^6$ N·mm:

Inner term = $\dfrac{4.6 \times 308.6 \times 10^6}{20 \times 2800 \times 400^2} = \dfrac{1419.6 \times 10^6}{8960 \times 10^6} = 0.1584$

$A_{st} = \dfrac{0.5 \times 20}{415}(1 - \sqrt{1-0.1584}) \times 2800 \times 400$

$= 0.02410 \times (1 - 0.9132) \times 1{,}120{,}000 = 0.02410 \times 0.0868 \times 1{,}120{,}000$

$A_{st}$ = 2342 mm²

Min steel = $0.12/100 \times 2800 \times 450 = 1512$ mm² ($D$ = 400 + 50 = 450 mm). Calculated $A_{st}$ (2342) > min (1512) ✓

Provide 10 bars of 16 mm dia: $A_{st,\text{prov}} = 10 \times 201 = 2010$ mm² — slightly less. Use 12 bars of 16 mm: $A_{st,\text{prov}} = 12 \times 201 = 2412$ mm² > 2342 ✓

Spacing = $2800/12 = 233$ mm c/c < min(3×400, 300) = 300 mm ✓

Step 7 — Development Length Check

For 16 mm Fe 415 bars in M20 concrete: $L_d = 47\phi = 47 \times 16 = 752$ mm

Available length = projection − end cover = $1200 - 50 = 1150$ mm

Available (1150 mm) > $L_d$ (752 mm) ✓ — Development length is satisfied
Step 8 — Overall Depth and $p_t$ Verification

$D = d + \text{cover} + \phi/2 = 400 + 50 + 8 = 458$ mm → round to $D = 460$ mm

Verify $p_t$: $p_t = \dfrac{100 \times 2412}{2800 \times 400} = 0.215\%$

From IS 456 Table 19, M20, $p_t = 0.215\%$: $\tau_c \approx 0.33$ MPa > $\tau_v$ (0.306 MPa) ✓

$D$ = 460 mm. All shear checks confirmed ✓
Final Design Summary
  • Footing size: 2800 mm × 2800 mm × 460 mm (L × B × D)
  • Effective depth $d$: 400 mm
  • Net upward pressure $q_u$: 153.06 kN/m²
  • Reinforcement (both ways): 12 bars of 16 mm dia (Fe 415) @ 233 mm c/c — $A_{st}$ = 2412 mm²
  • Clear cover: 50 mm (footing against soil)
  • PCC blinding: 75 mm M10 below footing
  • Punching shear: $\tau_v$ = 0.861 MPa < 1.118 MPa ✓
  • One-way shear: $\tau_v$ = 0.306 MPa < 0.33 MPa ✓
  • Development length: 1150 mm available > 752 mm required ✓
