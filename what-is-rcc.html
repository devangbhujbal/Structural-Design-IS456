1. Definition of RCC

Definition
Reinforced Cement Concrete (RCC) is a composite material in which concrete's relatively low tensile strength and ductility are compensated for by the inclusion of steel reinforcement bars (rebars), mesh, or fibres having higher tensile strength and ductility.

RCC is the most widely used construction material in India and across the world. Almost every structure you see around you — buildings, bridges, dams, flyovers — is made of RCC. It is governed in India by IS 456 : 2000, the code of practice for plain and reinforced concrete.

IS 456 : 2000 Reference
Clause 5 of IS 456:2000 defines the materials used in RCC, including cement, fine and coarse aggregates, water, admixtures, and reinforcement steel.

2. What is Concrete?

Concrete is a mixture of cement, fine aggregate (sand), coarse aggregate (gravel or crushed stone), and water. When water reacts with cement (a process called hydration), it forms a hard, stone-like material.

$$\text{Concrete} = \text{Cement} + \text{Fine Aggregate} + \text{Coarse Aggregate} + \text{Water}$$
Basic composition of concrete

Behaviour of Plain Concrete

Plain concrete (without steel) is:

  • Very strong in compression — it can resist heavy squeezing forces.
  • Very weak in tension — tensile strength is only about 1/10th of its compressive strength.
  • Brittle — it cracks suddenly without warning under tensile or flexural loads.
Key Insight
Plain concrete fails suddenly in bending because the tension zone at the bottom of a beam cracks. Steel reinforcement is placed in this tension zone to carry those forces safely.

3. Role of Steel Reinforcement

Steel bars (called rebars or reinforcement) are embedded in concrete to carry the tensile forces that concrete cannot resist. Steel has the following key properties:

Why Steel and Concrete Work Together
The coefficient of thermal expansion of steel (12 × 10⁻⁶ /°C) is very close to that of concrete (10–12 × 10⁻⁶ /°C). This means both materials expand and contract together with temperature changes, preventing cracking at the interface — making RCC an ideal composite.

5. Key Properties of RCC

Unit Weight

$$\gamma_{RCC} = 25 \text{ kN/m}^3$$
Unit weight of Reinforced Concrete as per IS 456:2000, Cl. 19.2

Modulus of Elasticity of Concrete

As per IS 456:2000, Clause 6.2.3.1:

$$E_c = 5000 \sqrt{f_{ck}} \text{ MPa}$$
Where $f_{ck}$ = characteristic compressive strength of concrete in MPa

For example, for M25 concrete: $E_c = 5000 \times \sqrt{25} = 5000 \times 5 = 25{,}000 \text{ MPa}$

Modular Ratio

The modular ratio $m$ relates the stiffness of steel to that of concrete:

$$m = \frac{E_s}{E_c} = \frac{280}{3\sigma_{cbc}}$$
As per IS 456:2000, Working Stress Method. $\sigma_{cbc}$ = permissible compressive stress in concrete

10. Common Mistakes to Avoid

These are the errors that most often cost marks in exams and cause re-work on site. Watch for them when applying this topic.

Common MistakeWhy it’s wrong / the correct approach
Reading “M20” as a mix ratio.The “M” stands for Mix, but the number is the characteristic compressive strength in N/mm² at 28 days on a 150 mm cube — not the 1:1.5:3 proportion.
Assuming concrete also carries tension.Concrete is strong in compression but its tensile strength is only about 10% of that. Steel is added precisely because plain concrete cracks and fails suddenly in tension.
Treating cover as optional or “extra concrete”.Nominal cover provides corrosion protection, fire resistance and bond. It is a code requirement that increases with exposure (IS 456 Cl. 26.4) — reducing it risks early reinforcement corrosion.
Believing more steel always means a stronger member.Beyond the balanced limit, extra tension steel makes a section over-reinforced and brittle. IS 456 caps steel (e.g. 4% gross area) for exactly this reason.
Confusing characteristic strength with average strength.Characteristic strength $f_{ck}$ is the value below which only 5% of test results are expected to fall — it is deliberately lower than the mean to account for variability.
Using plain mild-steel (Fe 250) bars by default.Modern RCC uses ribbed TMT bars — Fe 500 / Fe 500D. Design constants such as $x_{u,\max}/d$ and development length depend on the grade, so the grade must be fixed before designing.

11. Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does “M25” mean?

M = Mix, and 25 = the characteristic compressive strength of 25 N/mm² measured on a 150 mm cube at 28 days. It says nothing directly about the cement : sand : aggregate ratio.

What is the difference between RCC and PCC?

PCC (Plain Cement Concrete) has no reinforcement and is used where only compression acts (levelling course, bedding). RCC has steel bars to resist tension, shear and bending.

Why do concrete and steel work so well together?

They have nearly the same coefficient of thermal expansion, concrete grips the ribbed bars (bond), and the alkaline concrete forms a passive layer that protects the steel from rusting.

What is the minimum grade of concrete for RCC?

IS 456 Cl. 6.1.2 requires a minimum of M20 for reinforced concrete; M25 and above are common in urban and durability-critical work.

Which steel grade should I choose?

Fe 500 is the common default. Fe 500D (higher ductility) is preferred — and required by IS 13920 — for earthquake-resistant detailing.

Why is the steel placed near the bottom of a beam?

In a simply supported beam the bottom fibres are in tension. Steel is placed there to carry that tension; at continuous supports (hogging) the top is in tension instead.
