1. What is a Slender Column?

Definition — IS 456 : 2000, Clause 25.1.2
A column is classified as slender (long) when the slenderness ratio — the ratio of effective length to lateral dimension — exceeds 12 in either principal direction: $$\frac{l_{ex}}{D} \geq 12 \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{l_{ey}}{b} \geq 12$$ If both ratios are less than 12, the column is short.

In a slender column, lateral deflection under load causes additional bending moments beyond those from the applied loads. This is called the second-order effect or P-δ effect — the axial load $P$ acting through the lateral deflection $\delta$ generates an extra moment that must be added to the design moment.

Slender columns are common in open-frame buildings, tall structures, columns in open verandahs, and any situation where the unsupported height is large relative to the cross-section size. IS 456 Cl. 39.7 provides the additional moment method for handling this.

Never Ignore Slenderness
Designing a slender column as if it were short — ignoring the additional moment — can be dangerous. The additional moment can be a significant fraction of the applied moment, especially for very slender columns. Always check the slenderness ratio before choosing your design method.

2. Slenderness Classification

Short Column

  • $l_{ex}/D < 12$ and $l_{ey}/b < 12$
  • Second-order effects negligible
  • Design using IS 456 Cl. 39.3 (axial) or SP 16 interaction charts (eccentric)
  • No additional moment required

Slender Column

  • $l_{ex}/D \geq 12$ or $l_{ey}/b \geq 12$
  • Second-order (P-δ) effects significant
  • Additional moment $M_{add}$ must be computed and added
  • Design using IS 456 Cl. 39.7 + SP 16 interaction charts

3. Effective Length — IS 456 Table 28

The effective length $l_e$ of a column depends on the degree of rotational and translational restraint at its two ends. IS 456 Table 28 gives the effective length factor $\beta$ such that:

$$l_e = \beta \times l$$
$l$ = unsupported length of column; $\beta$ = effective length factor from IS 456 Table 28

Typical Building Columns
For columns in a typical braced multi-storey building (lateral loads resisted by shear walls), the effective length is usually taken as $0.65l$ to $0.80l$ depending on how rigidly the column is connected to the beams at top and bottom. For unbraced frames (no shear walls), effective lengths can exceed $l$ — IS 456 Annex E gives alignment charts (nomograms) for these cases.

4. Second-Order Effects and P-δ Moment

When a slender column carries an axial load $P_u$, it deflects laterally by an amount $\delta$ at mid-height. This lateral deflection causes the axial load to act eccentrically, generating an additional bending moment at mid-height:

Fig. 1 — In a slender column, lateral deflection δ causes axial load Pu to generate an additional bending moment Madd = Pu × δ at mid-height

This additional moment adds to any moment already present from eccentric loads or frame action. The total moment the column must be designed for is therefore larger than what the applied loads alone would suggest — and this difference can be very significant for highly slender columns.

5. Additional Moment Method — IS 456 Cl. 39.7

IS 456 uses the Additional Moment Method to account for second-order effects. Rather than a full geometric non-linear analysis, IS 456 provides simple formulae to compute the additional moment based on the slenderness ratio and axial load.

IS 456 : 2000 — Clause 39.7
The additional moments $M_{ax}$ and $M_{ay}$ for slender columns shall be computed as:
$$M_{ax} = \frac{P_u \cdot D}{2000} \left(\frac{l_{ex}}{D}\right)^2$$ $$M_{ay} = \frac{P_u \cdot b}{2000} \left(\frac{l_{ey}}{b}\right)^2$$
Additional moments about x and y axes — IS 456:2000, Cl. 39.7.1. $P_u$ in N, $D$ and $b$ in mm, $l_e$ in mm. Result is in N·mm.

These formulae are derived from the assumption that the mid-height deflection of a pin-ended column under uniform moment is:

$$\delta = \frac{l_e^2}{10 \times \frac{D}{d}} \times \frac{1}{2000}$$
Mid-height deflection assumption used in IS 456 Cl. 39.7 — simplified for practical design

6. Additional Moment Formulae — Simplified Form

The formulae can be written more compactly by defining the slenderness ratio:

$$\lambda_x = \frac{l_{ex}}{D} \qquad \lambda_y = \frac{l_{ey}}{b}$$
$$M_{ax} = \frac{P_u \cdot D \cdot \lambda_x^2}{2000}$$ $$M_{ay} = \frac{P_u \cdot b \cdot \lambda_y^2}{2000}$$
IS 456 Cl. 39.7.1 — additional moments. Only compute $M_{ax}$ if $\lambda_x \geq 12$, and only $M_{ay}$ if $\lambda_y \geq 12$.
Units Check
If $P_u$ is in kN and $D$, $b$, $l_e$ are in mm: $$M_{add} = \frac{P_u(\text{kN}) \times 10^3 \times D(\text{mm}) \times \lambda^2}{2000} \quad \text{N·mm}$$ Divide by $10^6$ to get kN·m. It is easiest to work entirely in N and mm to avoid unit errors.

7. Reduction Factor for Additional Moment

The full additional moment need not always be applied. IS 456 Cl. 39.7.1.1 allows a reduction factor $k$ to be applied to $M_{add}$ when the column carries significant axial load — because high axial load stiffens the column against lateral deflection.

IS 456 : 2000 — Clause 39.7.1.1
If the actual axial load $P_u$ is less than the balanced axial load $P_{uz}$, the additional moment may be reduced by the factor $k$:
$$k = \frac{P_{uz} - P_u}{P_{uz} - P_b} \leq 1.0$$
Reduction factor — IS 456 Cl. 39.7.1.1

Where:

  • $P_{uz}$ = maximum axial load capacity of the section (pure compression, zero moment)
  • $P_b$ = axial load corresponding to the balanced condition (concrete reaches 0.0035 strain simultaneously with steel yielding)
$$P_{uz} = 0.45 f_{ck} \cdot A_g + (0.75 f_y - 0.45 f_{ck}) \cdot A_{sc}$$
Maximum axial load capacity — IS 456 Cl. 39.7.1.1
Conservative Approach — $k = 1$
In preliminary design or when $P_u$ is small relative to $P_{uz}$, it is conservative and simpler to take $k = 1$ (i.e., apply the full additional moment without reduction). This is recommended for a first design pass.
$$M_{add,\text{design}} = k \cdot M_{add}$$
Reduced additional moment to use in design. For conservative design, use $k = 1$.

8. Total Design Moment

The total moment the column must be designed for is the sum of the applied moment and the additional (second-order) moment. IS 456 Cl. 39.7 specifies:

$$M_{ux,\text{total}} = M_{ux} + k \cdot M_{ax}$$ $$M_{uy,\text{total}} = M_{uy} + k \cdot M_{ay}$$
Total design moments about x and y axes — IS 456 Cl. 39.7

Where $M_{ux}$ and $M_{uy}$ are the applied factored moments from the structural analysis (from frame action, eccentric loading, etc.).

Minimum Initial Moment
Even if the analysis gives $M_{ux} = 0$ or $M_{uy} = 0$, the column must still be designed for at least the minimum eccentricity moment: $$M_{\min} = P_u \times e_{\min} \quad \text{where} \quad e_{\min} = \frac{l}{500} + \frac{D}{30} \geq 20 \text{ mm}$$ Use whichever is greater — $M_{\min}$ or the applied moment — as the starting moment before adding $M_{add}$.

Biaxial Bending Check

If the column is slender in both directions, it carries moments about both axes simultaneously. IS 456 Cl. 39.6 gives the biaxial bending check using an interaction equation:

$$\left(\frac{M_{ux,\text{total}}}{M_{ux1}}\right)^{\alpha_n} + \left(\frac{M_{uy,\text{total}}}{M_{uy1}}\right)^{\alpha_n} \leq 1.0$$
Biaxial bending interaction — IS 456 Cl. 39.6. $M_{ux1}$, $M_{uy1}$ = uniaxial moment capacities at the design $P_u$; $\alpha_n$ depends on $P_u/P_{uz}$.

9. Design Using SP 16 Interaction Charts

Once the total design moments $M_{ux,\text{total}}$ and $M_{uy,\text{total}}$ are known, the column section is designed using the SP 16 interaction charts — the same charts used for eccentrically loaded short columns.

SP 16 Chart Procedure for Slender Column
  1. Compute the non-dimensional axial load ratio: $\dfrac{P_u}{f_{ck} \cdot b \cdot D}$
  2. Compute the non-dimensional moment ratio: $\dfrac{M_{u,\text{total}}}{f_{ck} \cdot b \cdot D^2}$
  3. Choose the appropriate SP 16 chart for your $d'/D$ ratio (effective cover to steel / total depth)
  4. Read off $p/f_{ck}$ from the chart, then find $A_{sc} = p \times b \times D / 100$
  5. Check the biaxial interaction equation (IS 456 Cl. 39.6) if moments exist about both axes
SP 16 Chart Numbers for Reference
Charts 27–38 — Rectangular sections, $f_y$ = 250 MPa (Fe 250)
Charts 39–50 — Rectangular sections, $f_y$ = 415 MPa (Fe 415)
Charts 51–62 — Rectangular sections, $f_y$ = 500 MPa (Fe 500)
Within each group, charts are indexed by $d'/D$ = 0.05, 0.10, 0.15, 0.20

Practical Note on Slender Column Design
Because slender columns carry significant additional moments, they typically require more steel than a short column of the same size under the same axial load. It is often more economical to increase the column cross-section (reducing slenderness) rather than adding very heavy reinforcement.

11. Step-by-Step Design Procedure

  • Given Data Factored axial load $P_u$, applied moments $M_{ux}$ and $M_{uy}$, unsupported length $l$, end conditions, $f_{ck}$, $f_y$, assumed section size $b \times D$.
  • Find Effective Length $l_e$ $l_{ex} = \beta_x \times l$; $l_{ey} = \beta_y \times l$. Use IS 456 Table 28 for $\beta$.
  • Check Slenderness — Confirm Column is Slender $\lambda_x = l_{ex}/D$; $\lambda_y = l_{ey}/b$. If either $\geq 12$, column is slender — proceed. If both $< 12$, design as short column (IS 456 Cl. 39.3).
  • Compute Minimum Eccentricity Moment $e_{\min} = l/500 + D/30 \geq 20$ mm. $M_{\min} = P_u \times e_{\min}$. Use $M_{ux} = \max(M_{ux,\text{applied}},\; M_{\min})$.
  • Calculate Additional Moments If $\lambda_x \geq 12$: $M_{ax} = P_u \cdot D \cdot \lambda_x^2 / 2000$ If $\lambda_y \geq 12$: $M_{ay} = P_u \cdot b \cdot \lambda_y^2 / 2000$
  • Find Reduction Factor $k$ (or take $k = 1$) Assume trial steel percentage $p$. Compute $P_{uz} = 0.45 f_{ck} A_g + (0.75 f_y - 0.45 f_{ck}) A_{sc}$ and $P_b$ from SP 16. Then $k = (P_{uz} - P_u)/(P_{uz} - P_b) \leq 1$. For first pass, take $k = 1$.
  • Calculate Total Design Moments $M_{ux,\text{total}} = M_{ux} + k \cdot M_{ax}$; $M_{uy,\text{total}} = M_{uy} + k \cdot M_{ay}$
  • Design Using SP 16 Interaction Chart Compute $P_u/(f_{ck} bD)$ and $M_{u,\text{total}}/(f_{ck} bD^2)$. Read $p/f_{ck}$ from appropriate chart. Find $A_{sc} = p \cdot b \cdot D / 100$.
  • Biaxial Check (if moments exist about both axes) Find $P_{uz}$, $\alpha_n$. Check: $(M_{ux,\text{total}}/M_{ux1})^{\alpha_n} + (M_{uy,\text{total}}/M_{uy1})^{\alpha_n} \leq 1.0$
  • Select Bars and Design Lateral Ties Choose diameter and number of bars. Tie dia $\geq \phi_{main}/4$ or 6 mm. Tie spacing $\leq \min(b,\; 16\phi_{\min},\; 300)$ mm.
  • Verify Steel Limits and Draw Detailing Sketch Check $0.8\% \leq p \leq 6\%$ of $A_g$. Draw cross-section showing bar positions, cover, tie arrangement.

12. Worked Example

Problem Statement

Design a slender column for the following data:

  • Factored axial load $P_u$ = 1200 kN
  • Applied factored moment about x-axis $M_{ux}$ = 40 kN·m (from frame analysis)
  • Applied factored moment about y-axis $M_{uy}$ = 0 (uniaxial bending only)
  • Unsupported length $l$ = 6.0 m
  • End conditions: both ends effectively held in position and restrained against rotation → $\beta = 0.65$
  • Column size: $b$ = 300 mm, $D$ = 500 mm
  • Concrete: M25 ($f_{ck}$ = 25 MPa), Steel: Fe 415 ($f_y$ = 415 MPa)
  • Exposure: Mild (clear cover = 40 mm)
Step 1 — Effective Length

$\beta = 0.65$ (both ends fixed — IS 456 Table 28)

$l_{ex} = l_{ey} = 0.65 \times 6000 = 3900$ mm

$l_e$ = 3900 mm
Step 2 — Check Slenderness

$\lambda_x = l_{ex}/D = 3900/500 = 7.8 < 12$ ✓ (not slender about x-axis)

$\lambda_y = l_{ey}/b = 3900/300 = 13.0 \geq 12$ ← slender about y-axis

Column is slender about the y-axis (weak axis). $M_{ay}$ must be computed.
Step 3 — Minimum Eccentricity Moment

$e_{\min} = l/500 + D/30 = 6000/500 + 500/30 = 12 + 16.67 = 28.67$ mm

$M_{\min} = P_u \times e_{\min} = 1200 \times 10^3 \times 28.67 = 34.4 \times 10^6$ N·mm = 34.4 kN·m

Applied $M_{ux}$ = 40 kN·m $>$ $M_{\min}$ (34.4 kN·m) → use $M_{ux}$ = 40 kN·m

$M_{ux}$ = 40 kN·m (governs over $M_{\min}$)
Step 4 — Additional Moment (y-axis only, since $\lambda_x < 12$)

$M_{ay} = \dfrac{P_u \cdot b \cdot \lambda_y^2}{2000} = \dfrac{1200 \times 10^3 \times 300 \times 13.0^2}{2000}$

$= \dfrac{1200000 \times 300 \times 169}{2000} = \dfrac{60{,}840 \times 10^6}{2000}$

$M_{ay}$ = 30,420,000 N·mm = 30.42 kN·m
Step 5 — Reduction Factor $k$ (conservative: $k = 1$)

For the first design pass, take $k = 1$ (full additional moment applied).

$k$ = 1.0
Step 6 — Total Design Moments

About x-axis: $M_{ux,\text{total}} = 40 + 1.0 \times 0 = $ 40 kN·m (no $M_{ax}$ since $\lambda_x < 12$)

About y-axis: $M_{uy,\text{total}} = 0 + 1.0 \times 30.42 = $ 30.42 kN·m

Design moments: $M_{ux}$ = 40 kN·m, $M_{uy}$ = 30.42 kN·m (biaxial bending)
Step 7 — SP 16 Interaction Chart (x-axis, major axis governing)

Assume $d' = 40 + 6 + 12 = 58$ mm (cover + tie + half bar dia). $d'/D = 58/500 \approx 0.10$

$\dfrac{P_u}{f_{ck} \cdot b \cdot D} = \dfrac{1200 \times 10^3}{25 \times 300 \times 500} = \dfrac{1{,}200{,}000}{3{,}750{,}000} = 0.32$

$\dfrac{M_{ux}}{f_{ck} \cdot b \cdot D^2} = \dfrac{40 \times 10^6}{25 \times 300 \times 500^2} = \dfrac{40{,}000{,}000}{1{,}875{,}000{,}000} = 0.0213$

From SP 16 Chart 44 (Fe 415, $d'/D$ = 0.10): reading at (0.32, 0.0213) → $p/f_{ck} \approx 0.06$

$p = 0.06 \times 25 = 1.5\%$

$A_{sc} = 1.5 \times 300 \times 500 / 100 = $ 2250 mm²

Required $A_{sc}$ (x-axis) = 2250 mm²
Step 8 — SP 16 Chart for y-axis (weak axis with slenderness moment)

$d'/b = 58/300 \approx 0.20$. Use SP 16 Chart 47 (Fe 415, $d'/D$ = 0.20)

$\dfrac{M_{uy}}{f_{ck} \cdot D \cdot b^2} = \dfrac{30.42 \times 10^6}{25 \times 500 \times 300^2} = \dfrac{30{,}420{,}000}{1{,}125{,}000{,}000} = 0.0270$

From SP 16 Chart 47: reading at (0.32, 0.0270) → $p/f_{ck} \approx 0.08$

$p = 0.08 \times 25 = 2.0\%$

$A_{sc} = 2.0 \times 300 \times 500 / 100 = $ 3000 mm²

Required $A_{sc}$ (y-axis) = 3000 mm² — this governs
Step 9 — Select Bars

Provide 8 bars of 22 mm dia: $A_{sc,\text{prov}} = 8 \times 380 = 3040$ mm² $>$ 3000 mm² ✓

Check limits: $A_{sc,\text{min}} = 0.8\% \times 300 \times 500 = 1200$ mm²; $A_{sc,\text{max}} = 6\% \times 150000 = 9000$ mm²

8 bars of 22 mm dia (Fe 415) — $A_{sc}$ = 3040 mm² ✓
Step 10 — Lateral Ties

Tie dia = max(22/4, 6) = max(5.5, 6) = 6 mm → use 8 mm

Tie spacing = min($b$, $16\phi_{\min}$, 300) = min(300, 16×22, 300) = min(300, 352, 300) = 300 mm

8 mm ties @ 300 mm c/c
Final Design Summary
  • Column size: 300 mm × 500 mm
  • Slenderness: $\lambda_x$ = 7.8 (short), $\lambda_y$ = 13.0 (slender)
  • Additional moment: $M_{ay}$ = 30.42 kN·m about weak axis
  • Total design moments: $M_{ux}$ = 40 kN·m, $M_{uy}$ = 30.42 kN·m
  • Longitudinal steel: 8 bars of 22 mm dia (Fe 415) — $A_{sc}$ = 3040 mm²
  • Lateral ties: 8 mm dia @ 300 mm c/c
  • Clear cover: 40 mm (mild exposure)
  • All IS 456 checks satisfied ✓
