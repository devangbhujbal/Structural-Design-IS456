⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Treating the output as a final design. These results are for concept sizing and learning only. Construction drawings need full analysis (STAAD / ETABS) and a qualified engineer's sign-off.
  • Entering already-factored loads. Type in service (unfactored) dead and live loads — the calculator applies the 1.5 load factor itself. Pre-factoring double-counts the safety margin.
  • Expecting seismic and wind forces to be applied automatically. The location sets the design environment (seismic zone, basic wind speed), but the actual earthquake / wind load on the member must be added separately.
  • Setting a grade or cover below the exposure minimum. IS 456 Table 5 fixes the minimum grade, cover and w/c ratio by exposure — the tool nudges these up; don't override them downward.
  • Mixing up units. Spans and dimensions are in millimetres, loads in kN or kN/m as labelled. Entering metres where millimetres are expected throws the result off by 1000×.
  • Ignoring ductile detailing in seismic zones. In Zone III–V, IS 13920 detailing and Fe 500D are required — shown in the context but it must carry through to your detailing.
  • Skipping serviceability. Passing the strength check doesn't guarantee deflection or crack control — treat the span/depth output as a starting check, not a full serviceability verification.
❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Is this calculator a substitute for full structural analysis?
No. It is a preliminary and teaching tool that sizes members and steel quickly. Final designs must be confirmed with detailed analysis software and a licensed structural engineer.
Which IS codes does it follow?
IS 456 : 2000 for Limit State design, IS 1893 : 2016 for the seismic zone, IS 875 (Part 3) for basic wind speed, and IS 13920 for ductile detailing in higher seismic zones.
Why do I set the project location first?
The location derives the seismic zone and basic wind speed, which in turn drive the minimum material grade and detailing requirements that flow into every member you design.
Does it calculate earthquake and wind loads?
No — it establishes the design environment. The member forces due to earthquake or wind must be computed separately and added to the gravity load effects.
Are the loads I enter factored or unfactored?
Enter unfactored (characteristic) dead and live loads. The calculator applies the partial safety factor of 1.5 for the DL + LL combination internally.
Can I use these results for actual construction?
No — for preliminary design, checking and education only. Always verify with a detailed analysis and a qualified engineer before construction.
What's the difference between this and the Material Estimator?
This tool designs members (sizes and reinforcement). The Material Estimator works out material quantities and city-wise cost for the works.
